Scala

Chapter 1: Why Scala?

  1. A blend of functional and object-oriented statically-type language. The two programming styles have complementary strengths when it comes to scalability.
  2. Most things feel native, however, they're truly just library abstractions that can be extended and adapted.
  3. Two main ideas are basis for functional programming:
    1. Funtions are first-class values.
    2. Operations of a program should map input values to output rather than changing data in-place.
  4. Scala is: compatible, concise, high-level and statically-typed.
